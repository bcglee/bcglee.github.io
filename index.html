Newspaper Navigator
Hello! I am an incoming Assistant Professor in the University of Washington's Information School, where I am starting the Lab for Computing Cultural Heritage. Beginning in August, 2023, I will be the Kluge Fellow in Digital Studies at the Library of Congress. I recently completed my Ph.D. in the Paul G. Allen School for Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Washington, where I was advised by Professor Daniel Weld and was a member of the Lab for Human-AI Interaction. Previously, I was a 2020 Innovator in Residence at the Library of Congress. I am currently pursuing research toward my dissertation at the intersection of machine learning and human-computer interaction, with application to cultural heritage and the digital humanities. You can read more about my project, Newspaper Navigator, here (including a summary, publications, media coverage, and other resources). My Ph.D. research is supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship in machine learning.

Last fall, I taught a graduate seminar entitled, "Computing Cultural Heritage." You can find the syllabus here.

Before beginning my Ph.D., I was the inaugural Digital Humanities Associate Fellow at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum, as well as a Visiting Fellow in Harvard's History Department. I graduated from Harvard College in 2017 with a double major in Astrophysics & Mathematics and a minor in Physics.

I also write essays, which have appeared in or are forthcoming in WIRED, Jacobin, Current Affairs, Gawker, Real Life, and GoldFlakePaint. I maintain a semi-regular newsletter to share my writing as it is published.

On this website, you'll find my publications & code, CV, talks, selected honors, and contact info.

This website design is inspired by Space Jam's Website, which has remained untouched since 1996. The tiled background is a 100x100 pixel cutout of a Sloan Digital Sky Survey r-band image of the Globular Cluster M2 (SDSS run 2583, field 136, camcol 2). I studied this patch of sky for my undergraduate thesis with Professor Douglas Finkbeiner and Stephen Portillo.
