Newspaper Navigator
Hello! I am an incoming Assistant Professor in the University of Washington's Information School, where I am starting the Lab for Computing Cultural Heritage. Currently, I am a Kluge Fellow in Digital Studies at the Library of Congress. I recently completed my Ph.D. in the Paul G. Allen School for Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Washington, which was supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship in machine learning. Previously, I was a 2020 Innovator in Residence at the Library of Congress. You can read more about my project, Newspaper Navigator, here.

I am a co-editor of the forthcoming "Debates in DH" volume Cultures of Scale: Disciplines, Data, Labor, a general editor at Digital Humanities Quarterly, a member of the Digital Public Library of America’s Cultural Heritage Steering Committee, a founding affiliate of the University of Washington’s Center for the Advancement of Libraries, Museums, and Archives, an advisory board member of the Research Society of American Periodicals, and an editorial board member of the Nineteenth-century Data Collective.

I also write essays, which have appeared in WIRED, Current Affairs, Jacobin, Gawker, Real Life, Protean Magazine, and GoldFlakePaint, and are forthcoming in the Bennington Review and Bright Wall/Dark Room. I maintain a semi-regular newsletter to share my writing as it is published.

Before beginning my Ph.D., I was the inaugural Digital Humanities Associate Fellow at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum, as well as a Visiting Fellow in Harvard's History Department. I graduated from Harvard College in 2017 with a double major in Astrophysics & Mathematics and a minor in Physics.

This website design is inspired by Space Jam's Website, which has remained untouched since 1996. The tiled background is a 100x100 pixel cutout of a Sloan Digital Sky Survey r-band image of the Globular Cluster M2 (SDSS run 2583, field 136, camcol 2). I studied this patch of sky for my undergraduate thesis with Professor Douglas Finkbeiner and Stephen Portillo.
